Fibre optic internet: what you actually gain

Net Zone Team

Fibre carries data as light through glass. That one physical fact produces every advantage below: speed that doesn't decay over distance, immunity to electrical interference, and capacity that outlives whatever router you plug into it. Here's what that means in practice for a home or shop in Sreenagar.

Speed holds up in bad weather

Wireless last-mile links fade in heavy rain. Copper lines corrode and pick up noise from motors and power lines. Glass does neither. A fibre line delivers the same 50 Mbps during a July storm as on a clear day, which is exactly when everyone's home streaming.

Latency drops to single digits

Ping to BDIX servers over fibre sits around 2 to 8 ms on our network. Mobile data typically runs 30 to 60 ms and jumps around under load. For video calls that's the difference between talking over each other and a normal conversation. For gaming it decides whether you shoot first.

Uploads match your real life now

Old broadband assumed you only download. Now you back up phone photos, send 200 MB videos on WhatsApp, host video calls and push CCTV footage to the cloud. Fibre handles upload traffic properly, and that changes daily use more than the download number does.

The line outlives the package

The same strand of glass that carries 20 Mbps today can carry 1,000 Mbps later. Upgrading means changing equipment at the ends, without digging or rewiring your house. Buy the speed you need now and raise it with one phone call when life changes.

For a business, it's uptime money

A shop's POS terminal, a pharmacy's inventory lookup, an office VPN, cloud accounting: all of it stops when the line stops. Fibre's stability cuts those interruptions sharply. Add the BTRC downtime rule (pay 50% after 5 days down, 25% after 10, zero after 15) and your connectivity risk gets small and predictable.

What installation involves

A technician pulls a thin cable from the nearest pole or junction to your wall, mounts a small ONU box, and connects your router. At Net Zone the visit is free and usually done within an hour. The cable is thinner than a phone charger wire and follows walls neatly.

FAQ

Is fibre fragile?
The glass core sits inside protective jacketing that handles normal bending. Sharp folds and furniture crushing it are the main risks, and a snapped drop cable is a quick, free repair call.

Does fibre work during power cuts?
The line itself carries light regardless, but your ONU and router need electricity. A small UPS or router power bank (৳1,500 or so) keeps internet alive through load-shedding.

Why does my phone show less than the package speed?
